Welcome to Swecha Club

Freedom Fest

MVGR is celebrating 10 years of the SWECHA LEARNING CENTRE by conducting a 3-day FREEDOM FEST with the motto of celebrating the art of learning, exploring, and sharing software knowledge.

Workshops

The 3-day fest comprises workshops on Linux administration, Android development, Botpress, Arduino for IoT, Websites with WordPress, and PHP for WordPress.

Chief Guest

Mr. Sai Kiran, Software Developer at Cap Gemini, has been invited as the chief guest for the inaugural function of the 3-day Freedom Fest.

Addressing Students

Mr. Sai Kiran, chief guest of the function, addressed the students about the importance of the syllabus of the freedom fest and conveyed his best wishes to all the participants.

Emphasis on Productivity

Dr. Y.M.C. Sekhar, Vice Principal at MVGR, emphasized that this is not just a platform to read something and go, but a place where youngsters become productive by working with teams and exploring themselves.

Importance of Learning Centers

Mr. M. Mohan Kumar, Dean-Students, addressed the student participants and emphasized the importance of learning centers in engineering colleges like SWECHA. He highlighted how through such software fests, students can gain valuable experience by using open source software and engaging in discussions with fellow students.

Freedom of Technology

Mr. M. Vamsi Krishna, Convenor at MVGR SLC, highlighted that SWECHA AP is a place where you can find yourselves by experiencing the freedom of technology through experimentation. He also introduced some of the combined initiatives by SWECHA AP and MVGR SWECHA LEARNING CENTER like BALA SWECHA, FREEDOM BOX, and MVGR MOODLE.

Contemporary Issues

Mr. G. Phanindra, Student Event Coordinator, discussed contemporary issues regarding software and how they learned using open sources.

Inaugural Event

For the inaugural event, Dr. Y.M.C. Sekhar (Vice-principal), Mr. S. Mohan Kumar (Dean-Students), Dr. G. Suvarna Kumar (Associate Professor, CSE), Mr. M. Vamsi Krishna (Convener, MVGR SLC), and G. Phanindra (Event Coordinator), along with other faculty members and student participants from nearby colleges, were present.
